# Diversity, Equity and Inclusion (DEI) Policy

Document ID: POL-DEI-007
Owner: HR / DEI Council
Status: Approved
Version: 2.0
Last Updated: 2026-01-20
Applies to: All Employees, All Towers

## 1. Commitment

Capgemini is committed to building an inclusive workplace where employees of all backgrounds, identities, and abilities are treated with fairness, dignity, and equal opportunity across hiring, compensation, promotion, and daily treatment.

## 2. Equal Opportunity

1. Recruitment, promotion, and compensation decisions are based on merit, skill, and performance, without regard to gender, religion, caste, ethnicity, disability, sexual orientation, gender identity, age, or marital status.
2. Reasonable workplace accommodations are available for employees with disabilities — requests can be raised confidentially with HRBP or through HR Portal > Accessibility Support.

## 3. Employee Resource Groups (ERGs)

1. Capgemini supports several voluntary Employee Resource Groups (Women's network, LGBTQ+ ally network, Disability inclusion network, and others) — details and how to join are published on HR Portal > DEI > Communities.
2. ERGs are open to allies as well as members of the represented group.

## 4. Reporting Discrimination

1. Any discrimination, bias, or exclusionary behavior should be reported through the Ethics Helpline (see Grievance and Ethics Policy POL-ETH-006), with the same anti-retaliation protections applying.
2. DEI Council periodically reviews aggregated (anonymized) data trends to identify systemic issues, separate from individual case handling.

## 5. Inclusive Practices Expected of All Employees

1. Use inclusive language in written and verbal communication, especially in client-facing and recruitment contexts.
2. Be mindful of accessibility in shared documents and presentations (alt text, readable color contrast, captioning for recorded sessions where feasible).
3. Participate respectfully in cross-cultural project teams; Capgemini's global delivery model means most projects involve colleagues across multiple countries, cultures, and working norms.
