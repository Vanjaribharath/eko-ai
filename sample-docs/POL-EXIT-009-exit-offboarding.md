# Exit and Offboarding Policy

Document ID: POL-EXIT-009
Owner: HR Operations
Status: Approved
Version: 3.3
Last Updated: 2026-02-08
Applies to: All Employees, All Towers

## 1. Resignation Process

1. Submit resignation through HR Portal > Separation > Initiate Resignation; this triggers the formal notice period as per your appointment letter.
2. Your reporting manager and HRBP are notified automatically; a discussion typically follows regarding handover planning and last working day confirmation.

## 2. Notice Period

1. Standard notice period is 60-90 days depending on grade, as specified in your appointment letter (see Terms and Conditions POL-TNC-001, Section 5).
2. Notice period buyout (paying in lieu of serving notice) may be permitted at company discretion, calculated per the formula in Payroll Policy (POL-PAY-014), Section 6.
3. Leave during notice period is permitted but requires manager approval and should not compromise handover commitments.

## 3. Knowledge Transfer (KT) and Handover

1. A formal KT plan is expected for any role with ongoing project responsibilities, documented and shared with your manager and team.
2. Sole-owned components, undocumented processes, or critical credentials must be transitioned with extra care — flag these early to your manager so adequate KT time is planned.

## 4. Clearance Process

1. IT Clearance: return all assets (laptop, access card, peripherals) — see Asset Policy (POL-AST-005), Section 4.
2. Finance Clearance: settle any outstanding advances, expense claims, or company credit card dues.
3. Admin Clearance: return any physical access cards, parking passes, or office-issued items.
4. Project Clearance: confirm handover completion and access revocation from project systems, coordinated by your Project Manager.

## 5. Full and Final (F&F) Settlement

1. Processed typically within 45 days of your last working day, contingent on all clearances above being completed.
2. Includes: pending salary, leave encashment (Earned Leave balance), any pending reimbursements, less any recoveries (notice period shortfall, asset damage, advances).
3. Form 16 and relieving letter are issued after F&F settlement is processed, downloadable from HR Portal > Separation > Documents.

## 6. Rehire Eligibility

1. Employees who exit in good standing (completed notice period, clean clearance, no policy violations) are generally eligible for rehire consideration in the future.
2. Employees terminated for cause or who exit with incomplete/negative clearance may have rehire eligibility restricted — confirm with HR if relevant to your situation.

## 7. Post-Exit Obligations

Confidentiality, non-solicitation, and intellectual property obligations from the Terms and Conditions (POL-TNC-001) continue to apply after your last working day — see that document, Sections 2 and 3, for details.
