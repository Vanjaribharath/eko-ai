# IT Asset and Equipment Policy

Document ID: POL-AST-005
Owner: IT Asset Management
Status: Approved
Version: 4.0
Last Updated: 2026-02-01
Applies to: All Employees, All Towers

## 1. Asset Issuance

1. Standard issue for most roles is one laptop; additional equipment (external monitor, headset, docking station) depends on role and work-from-home arrangement, requested via IT Helpdesk > Asset Request.
2. Assets remain company property at all times; employees are responsible for reasonable care and immediate reporting of damage, loss, or theft.

## 2. Acceptable Use of Assets

1. Company laptops are intended primarily for work use; reasonable personal use (checking personal email, browsing) is permitted but must not violate the IT Security and Acceptable Use Policy (POL-SEC-003).
2. Installing unauthorized software, disabling endpoint security/antivirus, or attempting to bypass VPN or MDM controls is prohibited and may trigger an automatic security alert to InfoSec.
3. Do not connect unknown or personal USB drives to company laptops — many projects have USB port restrictions enabled by default for this reason.

## 3. Loss, Theft, or Damage

1. Report immediately to IT Helpdesk > Lost/Stolen Device — delays in reporting can affect both data security response time and any device replacement process.
2. For lost/stolen devices, also file a police report (FIR) where required by local law or client contract; IT Helpdesk will advise if this applies to your case.
3. Accidental damage is generally covered under standard asset insurance; repeated damage may result in a cost recovery charge depending on circumstances.

## 4. Return of Assets

1. All company assets (laptop, access card, any peripherals) must be returned during offboarding, typically on or before your last working day, coordinated through IT Helpdesk > Asset Return.
2. Full and Final settlement (see Payroll Policy POL-PAY-014, Section 6) is contingent on asset return clearance.

## 5. Remote/Field Asset Handling

1. For roles involving travel or field work, additional asset tracking requirements may apply — confirm with your Project Manager.
2. Never leave a company laptop unattended in a public space, vehicle, or unsecured location, even briefly.
