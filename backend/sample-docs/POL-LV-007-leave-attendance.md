# Leave and Attendance Policy

Document ID: POL-LV-007
Owner: HR Operations
Status: Approved
Version: 4.4
Last Updated: 2026-01-15
Applies to: All Employees, All Towers

## 1. Leave Types and Annual Entitlement

1. Earned Leave (EL) — 18 days per year, accrued monthly at 1.5 days/month, carry-forward up to 45 days, encashable on exit.
2. Casual Leave (CL) — 7 days per year, for short personal needs, cannot be carried forward beyond the calendar year.
3. Sick Leave (SL) — 7 days per year, medical certificate required if availed for more than 2 consecutive days.
4. Maternity Leave — 26 weeks as per the Maternity Benefit Act, applicable for the first two children.
5. Paternity Leave — 10 working days, to be availed within 3 months of childbirth.
6. Bereavement Leave — 5 working days for immediate family loss.
7. Compensatory Off — granted for approved weekend/holiday work, must be availed within 60 days of accrual.

## 2. Applying for Leave

1. All leave is applied through the HR Portal under Leave > Apply.
2. Leave longer than 3 working days should be applied at least 5 working days in advance where possible, except sick leave.
3. Approval is routed to your reporting manager; project commitments should be discussed with your Project Manager separately for planning purposes.

## 3. Attendance and Working Hours

1. Standard working hours are 9 hours/day inclusive of a 1-hour break, aligned to project/client timezone requirements.
2. Attendance is tracked via swipe (office-based) or the Attendance Portal (remote/hybrid), and must be regularized within 3 working days if missed.
3. Persistent unregularized attendance gaps may result in Loss of Pay (see Payroll Policy POL-PAY-014, Section 6).

## 4. Work From Home (WFH) and Hybrid Policy

1. WFH eligibility and frequency depend on project and client policy — confirm with your Project Manager, as some client contracts mandate onsite presence.
2. Remote work requires a secure, private workspace; client data must never be visible to non-employees (family members, roommates) during screen-shares or calls.
3. VPN must remain connected for the full working session when accessing project systems remotely.

## 5. Holidays

1. Capgemini follows a list of restricted and gazetted holidays published annually on the HR Portal, varying slightly by work location.
2. Employees can typically choose 2-3 restricted holidays from a list in addition to fixed gazetted holidays.

## 6. Common Questions

1. Leave during notice period: permitted but subject to manager approval and project handover commitments; cannot reduce the notice period itself.
2. Leave without pay (LWP): available for extended personal circumstances beyond entitled leave, requires HRBP approval.
3. Negative leave balance: allowed up to a small buffer (varies by leave type); beyond that, days are marked LOP automatically.
