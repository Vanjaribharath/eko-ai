# Grievance Redressal and Ethics Helpline Policy

Document ID: POL-ETH-006
Owner: Corporate Compliance / HR
Status: Approved
Version: 3.1
Last Updated: 2026-01-12
Applies to: All Employees, All Towers

## 1. Purpose

This policy provides a clear, confidential path for raising workplace concerns, ethical violations, harassment complaints, or general grievances, with protection against retaliation for good-faith reports.

## 2. Types of Concerns Covered

1. Harassment or discrimination (including POSH-related complaints).
2. Conflicts of interest, bribery, or corruption.
3. Confidentiality or data security violations witnessed (not just experienced personally).
4. General workplace grievances: unfair treatment, policy disputes, manager conflicts unresolved through direct conversation.

## 3. How to Raise a Concern

1. For general grievances: first attempt resolution directly with your manager or HRBP; most issues resolve faster this way.
2. For sensitive matters (harassment, ethics violations, retaliation concerns): use the confidential Ethics Helpline, accessible via HR Portal > Ethics Helpline, available 24x7 and allows anonymous reporting if preferred.
3. POSH-specific complaints go to the Internal Committee (IC), with contact details published on HR Portal > POSH > Internal Committee Members.

## 4. What Happens After You Report

1. Ethics Helpline reports are triaged within 2 working days and assigned to an appropriate investigator, independent of your direct reporting chain where there's any conflict of interest.
2. You will receive a case reference number to track status, even for anonymous reports, through a secure portal.
3. Investigations are kept confidential to the extent possible; only those with a genuine need to know are involved.

## 5. Anti-Retaliation Guarantee

1. Capgemini's policy strictly prohibits retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith, even if the investigation does not substantiate the complaint.
2. Suspected retaliation should itself be reported immediately through the Ethics Helpline as a separate, high-priority case.

## 6. Self-Reporting and Amnesty

1. Employees who proactively self-report a policy violation (for example, an accidental confidentiality breach) before it is discovered are treated significantly more leniently than those found to have concealed an issue.
2. This applies to security incidents as well — see IT Security Policy (POL-SEC-003), Section 6, and the Code of Conduct Policy (POL-COC-001), Section 3.
