# Expense and Business Travel Policy

Document ID: POL-EXP-011
Owner: Finance / Travel Desk
Status: Approved
Version: 5.5
Last Updated: 2026-02-05
Applies to: All Employees, All Towers

## 1. Expense Claims — General Rules

1. All expense claims are submitted through the Expense Portal with original receipts/invoices attached as scanned images or PDFs.
2. Claims must be submitted within 30 days of the expense being incurred; claims older than 60 days require additional manager and Finance approval.
3. Personal expenses unrelated to work are never reimbursable, including personal meals outside approved travel, entertainment, or non-work-related purchases.

## 2. Business Travel

1. All business travel (client-site visits, conferences, training) must be booked through the approved Travel Desk/portal, not independently booked and reimbursed, except in pre-approved emergency situations.
2. Travel class entitlement (flight class, train class) depends on grade and distance — refer to the Travel Entitlement Matrix on the HR Portal under Travel > Entitlements.
3. Hotel bookings should use the corporate-rate hotel list where available; per-diem limits apply for meals during travel, varying by city/country.

## 3. Local Conveyance and Client-Site Travel

1. Daily commute to a regular office or fixed client site is not reimbursable.
2. Travel between multiple client sites during work hours, or travel for project-related meetings outside your regular work location, is reimbursable with receipts.
3. Mileage/fuel claims for personal vehicle use require prior manager approval and adherence to the per-kilometer rate published on the HR Portal.

## 4. International Travel

1. Visa processing is coordinated through the Travel Desk; initiate requests at least 4-6 weeks before travel for visa-requiring destinations.
2. Travel insurance is automatically provided for all approved international business travel.
3. Foreign currency advances can be requested through the Travel Desk up to 5 working days before departure.

## 5. Approval Workflow

1. Domestic travel under the standard entitlement: Project Manager approval is sufficient.
2. International travel or travel exceeding standard entitlement: requires additional approval from the Delivery/Engagement Manager.
3. Expense claims above a certain threshold (varies by grade) require second-level Finance approval before reimbursement.

## 6. Common Issues

1. Receipt lost: a self-declaration form is available on the Expense Portal for claims under a small threshold; larger claims without receipts are generally not reimbursable.
2. Claim rejected: check the rejection comment on the Expense Portal; most rejections are due to missing receipts, expense category mismatch, or missing approval. Resubmit with corrections.
3. Reimbursement delay: claims are typically processed within 10 working days of final approval; if delayed beyond 15 working days, raise a ticket with the Finance Helpdesk.
