# New to Project Guide — For Experienced Employees Moving Project

Document ID: ONB-NTP-006
Owner: Resource Management Group (RMG) / Project Management Office (PMO)
Status: Approved
Version: 2.3
Last Updated: 2026-02-18
Applies to: Experienced Employees moving between projects, All Towers

## 1. Who This Applies To

This guide is for employees who already work at Capgemini and are being released from one project (or moved off the bench) onto a new project. It is different from the New Joiner guide, which covers people who are brand new to the company.

## 2. Before You Move

1. Your Resource Manager (RM) will share the new project's Statement of Work (SOW) summary, client name, tower, and start date.
2. Complete the project-specific Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) acknowledgement if required by the new client — this is separate from your general employment NDA.
3. Ensure clearance/offboarding from the previous project: return any project-specific hardware, revoke old project access, and complete a knowledge transfer (KT) if you were a sole owner of any component.

## 3. First Week on the New Project

1. Get access provisioned: repository, project tools (Jira/Confluence/ServiceNow depending on tower), VPN profile for the client network if applicable, and any client-specific tools.
2. Attend the project induction session run by the Project Manager or Tech Lead, covering architecture overview, team structure, ways of working (Agile/Scrum cadence), and current sprint context.
3. Review the project's specific Confidentiality Addendum, if the client has one beyond the standard Code of Conduct policy (POL-COC-001).
4. Shadow a team member for the first 3-5 working days before taking independent tickets, unless your Tech Lead indicates otherwise.

## 4. Differences From Being a Fresh New Joiner

1. You do not need to redo Day 1 IT setup (VPN, MFA, email) — that already exists. You only need new project-specific access added.
2. You do not need to redo company-wide mandatory trainings already completed and not yet expired (check Learning Portal > My Trainings > Status before redoing anything).
3. You will likely need project-specific trainings unique to the new client or technology stack — these are assigned by the Project Manager within the first week.

## 5. Common Issues When Switching Projects

1. Old project's VPN profile sometimes conflicts with new one — raise an IT Helpdesk ticket under Access Request > VPN Profile Conflict if you cannot connect.
2. Email distribution lists from the old project may take a few days to update — ask your RM to confirm you're removed from old lists and added to new ones.
3. If your new project requires tower realignment (e.g., moving from ITS to Digital), your reporting manager and cost center will update in the HR system, which can take 5-7 working days to reflect.

## 6. Who to Contact

1. Project access issues: IT Helpdesk
2. Compensation or designation questions related to the move: your HR Business Partner
3. Project context, ways of working, expectations: your new Project Manager or Tech Lead
4. Resourcing/bench timeline questions: your Resource Manager
