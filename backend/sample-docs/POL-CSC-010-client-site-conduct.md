# Client Site Conduct and Visitor Policy

Document ID: POL-CSC-010
Owner: Delivery Excellence / Corporate Compliance
Status: Approved
Version: 2.2
Last Updated: 2026-02-16
Applies to: Employees working at Client Sites, All Towers

## 1. General Conduct at Client Sites

1. Represent Capgemini professionally at all times — client perception of Capgemini is shaped heavily by on-site team conduct.
2. Follow the client's local site rules (badge display, restricted areas, dress code) in addition to Capgemini policies; where client rules are stricter, follow the stricter rule.
3. Never discuss Capgemini's internal matters (other clients, internal restructuring, compensation) in client-audible spaces.

## 2. Device and Photography Rules at Client Sites

1. Many client sites have stricter device policies than Capgemini's own offices — some prohibit personal phones entirely in certain zones (e.g., trading floors, data centers, secure operations areas).
2. Photography of any kind is prohibited at client sites unless explicitly permitted in writing by both the client and your Capgemini Project Manager — this is in addition to, not a replacement for, the no-photography rule in the Code of Conduct Policy (POL-COC-001).
3. Laptops and devices used at client sites are subject to client-side security scanning in many engagements; do not attempt to bypass or disable any client-mandated security agent.

## 3. Badge and Access Management

1. Client site badges must be displayed visibly at all times and never lent to another individual, including other Capgemini colleagues.
2. Report a lost client badge to both the client's security desk and your Capgemini Project Manager immediately — delays can affect the whole project team's access if the client locks down access as a precaution.
3. Tailgating (entering secure areas by following someone else's badge swipe) is strictly prohibited and treated as a serious security violation by most clients.

## 4. Working Hours and On-Site Expectations

1. On-site working hours, holidays, and break schedules follow the client's site calendar where it differs from the standard Capgemini calendar — confirm specifics with your Project Manager.
2. Remote work eligibility while officially "on client site" engagement varies entirely by client contract; some mandate full on-site presence, others allow hybrid arrangements.

## 5. Incidents at Client Sites

1. Any security incident, confidentiality concern, or conduct issue at a client site should be reported simultaneously to your Capgemini Project Manager and through the standard InfoSec/Ethics channels described in POL-SEC-003 and POL-ETH-006 — client sites do not have a separate Capgemini reporting process.
2. Workplace safety incidents (injury, fire, evacuation) follow the client site's own emergency protocol, typically briefed during site induction.
