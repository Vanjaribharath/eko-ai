# Performance Management and Appraisal Policy

## 1. Performance Cycle Overview

1. The annual performance cycle runs April to March, aligned with the financial year.
2. Goal setting happens at the start of the cycle (April-May) through the Performance Portal, typically 3-5 goals aligned to project and individual development objectives.
3. Mid-year check-in (informal, typically September-October) and final year-end review (February-March) are the two formal touchpoints, alongside ongoing informal feedback.

## 2. Rating Scale

1. Ratings typically follow a 5-point scale: Outstanding, Exceeds Expectations, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement, Unsatisfactory — exact scale labels may be communicated each cycle via HR Portal announcements.
2. Ratings are calibrated across teams by HRBPs and Delivery Leadership to ensure consistency, which is why individual ratings may be adjusted slightly from a manager's initial recommendation.

## 3. Who Provides Input

1. Your reporting manager is the primary rater, but 360-degree feedback may be collected from project stakeholders (Project Manager, peers, client stakeholders where applicable).
2. Self-assessment is a mandatory part of the process — completed by the employee before the manager's review, through the Performance Portal.

## 4. Link to Compensation

1. Final ratings influence annual salary revision and bonus payout, processed alongside the broader compensation cycle described in the Payroll Policy (POL-PAY-014), Section 5.
2. Promotions are considered during the same cycle but follow a separate nomination and review process, not automatic from rating alone.

## 5. If You Disagree With Your Rating

1. Discuss directly with your reporting manager first — most disagreements stem from miscommunication about goal interpretation.
2. If unresolved, you may raise a formal review request to your HRBP within 15 days of rating communication.
3. The Performance Portal maintains a record of all goal-setting and feedback conversations, useful evidence for any review discussion.

## 6. Performance Improvement Plans (PIP)

1. A PIP may be initiated for employees consistently rated Needs Improvement or below, with clear, time-bound objectives (typically 60-90 days).
2. PIPs are managed jointly by the reporting manager and HRBP, with regular check-ins during the PIP period.
